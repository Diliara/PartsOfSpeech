Definition: a word that can function by itself as a noun phrase and that refers either to the participants in the discourse (e.g., I, you ) or to someone or something mentioned elsewhere in the discourse (e.g., she, it, this ). Types of pronouns include:

Personal: take the place of common and proper nouns

  • First person singular: I, me
  • First person plural: we, us
  • Second person: you
  • Third person singular: she, her, he, him, it
  • Third person plural: they, them

Relative: relate the subordinate adjective clauses to the rest of the sentence

  • That, which, who, whom, whose, whichever, woever, whomever

Demonstrative: represent a thing or things

  • Nearby singular: this
  • Nearby plural: these
  • Far away singular: that
  • Far away plural: those

Indefinite: refers to something that is unspecified

  • Singluar: anybody, anyone, anything, each, either, everybody, everyone, everything, neither, nobody, no one, nothing, one, somebody, something, someone
  • Plural: both, few, many, several
  • Singular or plural: all, any, most, none, some

Reflexive: end in self or selves

  • First person: myself / ourselves
  • Second person: yourself / yourselves
  • Third person: himself, herself, itself / themselves

Interrogative: used to ask questions

  • What, who, which, whom, whose
